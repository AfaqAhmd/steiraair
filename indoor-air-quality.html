What is Indoor Air Quality (IAQ)?

Indoor air quality, IAQ is the quality of the air that we breathe inside the buildings such as homes, offices, schools, and other enclosed spaces. Achieving and maintaining a good quality of the air. It’s important to create a healthy and comfortable environment for residential and commercial dwellings.

Indoor air quality is one of the most important aspects of our daily lives. However, we spend most of the time indoors for air quality can have adverse effects on our health, comfort, and overall well-being, as well as on the environment that we live in.

Negative Impacts of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Health Impacts: Or indoor air quality can lead to various health-related risks, ranging from mild to severe conditions.

Causes of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Inadequate ventilation: Insufficient fresh air due to poor ventilation system can build up air pollutants in the environment


Indoor pollutants: Most common indoor Politan, such as dust, pet dander, mold pollen, and other volatile organic compounds VOCs release some chemicals from the cleaning products, such as furniture can pollute the air


Moisture and mold: Excessive moisture in your home, or office, can lead to the growth of mold and mildew, which can release spores into the air and can lead to respiratory problems and allergies


Building materials: Some of our building materials, including asbestos, lead-based paint, and formaldehyde-containing products, release some toxins in the air and pollute the environment.


HVAC System Issues: Improperly maintained HVAC Systems and dirty coils causes poor air quality.
